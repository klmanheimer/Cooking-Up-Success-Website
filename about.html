Lore

Vinny Vortex wasn’t always Vinny. His real name was Victor Voss, a washed-up magician turned street performer in a rundown corner of Las Vegas. Once hailed as “The Prince of Illusions,” Vinny had the kind of fame that burns fast and disappears faster. One night, during his final show at a dingy casino, his greatest illusion failed — not on stage, but in real life: his partner left, his act bombed, and the crowd threw chicken wings instead of roses. Rock bottom had a sticky floor.Desperate and broke, he wandered into a 24-hour market, trying to figure out what came next. There, he stumbled on something strange in the discount bin — a pizza cookbook called "The Alchemy of Dough", and tucked inside, a handwritten shopping list with a post-it note that read: "For when the magic fades — try mozzarella." That night, Vinny used what little cash he had to buy everything on the list: fresh yeast, buffalo mozzarella, fire-roasted tomatoes, and oddly… rosemary and cinnamon. He baked his first pizza in an abandoned food truck behind his motel — and it was incredible.That was the moment it hit him: his next trick wouldn’t be on stage, it would be on a plate. Vinny cleaned out the truck, painted flames on the side, and opened “Vinny Vortex’s Pizza Portal” — a traveling pizza show that combined fire-spinning, sleight-of-hand, and artisan pizza tossed so high it disappeared (sometimes literally).The shopping list? Still with him. Taped to the dashboard, greasy and coffee-stained, it's been updated with notes like: “Add chili flakes: reminds me I’m alive.” “Never skimp on crust. That’s where the soul lives.” Vinny’s pizza isn’t just food — it’s redemption, entertainment, and proof that even when the magic’s gone, you can still rise.
